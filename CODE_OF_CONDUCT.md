# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

Mycel is a spec-first technical project. We expect discussion to stay clear, respectful, and evidence-based.

Examples of behavior that help this project:

- focusing on the protocol, implementation, tests, or documented scope
- explaining disagreements with concrete reasoning
- being explicit about assumptions, risks, and tradeoffs
- correcting mistakes without personal attacks
- keeping review and issue discussion constructive and technically relevant

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, intimidation, or discrimination
- insulting, demeaning, or hostile language directed at people
- repeated bad-faith argument after the technical point is already clear
- deliberate derailment of technical discussion
- publishing private information without permission
- spam or promotional content unrelated to the project

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, review comments, and other project spaces.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that do not follow this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation for repeated or severe violations.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainers privately instead of starting a public confrontation.

When reporting, include:

- where the incident happened
- links or screenshots if available
- what happened
- any immediate safety or moderation concern

## Maintainer Guidance

When enforcing this policy, prefer actions that restore productive technical collaboration quickly and proportionally.
